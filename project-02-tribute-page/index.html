Bettie Page's Biography

  • 1923 - Bettie was born on April 22, in Nashville, Tennessee. She was the second child of Walter Roy Page & Edna Mae Pirtle’s six children.
  • 1943 - In February 1943, Bettie married her boyfriend of two years, Billy Neal. After earning her Bachelor of Arts degree from Peabody College, she moved to San Francisco to be with him. This was where she got her first modeling job at a local furrier where she modeled fur coats for clients.
  • 1947 - Bettie divorced Billy Neal. For the next few years she traveled from San Francisco to Nashville to Miami, even to Port-au-Prince, Haiti.
  • 1950 = Bettie met Jerry Tibbs on the Coney Island shore. He was a police officer with an interest in photography. Tibbs took pictures of Bettie and put together her first pinup portfolio.
  • 1955 - She was the centerfold in Playboy’s January issue. She was even the name of “Girl with the Perfect Figure”.
  • 1953 - She auditioned for an apprenticeship at Sea Cliff Summer Theater in Long Island where she studied acting under the tutelage of Herbert Berghoff. She secured several roles in various New York productions and in various television appearances, which included her off-Broadway productions, Time is a Thief and Sunday Costs Five Pesos and The Jackie Gleason show.
  • 1958 - On November 26, Bettie married her second husband, Armond Walterson, but divorced him in 1963.
